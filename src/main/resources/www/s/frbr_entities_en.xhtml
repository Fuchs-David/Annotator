Specification of FRBR entities

Work
Instances categorized as belonging to the FRBR class Work are concepts identified in artistic and intellectual expressions. The substance of a work is an idea.
A collection of works (e.g. a periodical) is a work as well as its members.
A work is a product of an intellectual process of which we have only indirect evidence (e.g. its expression, work order, etc.).
Expression
Instances categorized as belonging to the FRBR class Expression are intellectual or artistic realisations of works in the form of identifiable immaterial objects. Expressions cannot exist without a physical carrier, but do not depend on any single carrier. They can exist on any number of carriers simultaneously.
Potential carriers of an expression also include human memory.
Any change in form might create a new expression (e.g. alpha-numeric notation to spoken word) and it might require knowing the genre to identify features which are essential to the expression (e.g. typeface and page layout).
A collection of expressions (e.g. an anthology of poems) is a an expression as well as the members of the collection themselves.
Manifestation
Instances categorized as belonging to the FRBR class Manifestation represent all items that have been created as part of a series (e.g. an edition of a book). The item must be identifiable as a copy belonging to a manifestation, but does not need to exhibit all the features or traits of the manifestation (e.g. due to production events).
Physical objects (e.g. a manuscript or draft) not intended to be produced in multiple copies are still considered to be manifestations.
Item
Instances categorized as belonging to the FRBR class Item are physical copies - exemplars of a single manifestation.
